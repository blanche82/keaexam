Helping designers get on the same page

Design better with the visual toolkit aligning understanding and guiding design process.

As UX professionals often have diverse backgrounds, the terminology they use to refer to design concepts can be ambiguous. Thus they might not always be on the same page while working together.

We believe that they can get on the same page, however, and achieve better outcomes with the visual toolkit guiding them through the process.

What it is?

You are probably familiar with the British Design Council’s Double Diamond, IDEO’s human centered design ideology, d.school’s Design Thinking process, or the NN Group’s way to define usefulness in UX design. We are developing a visual toolkit enabling designers to harness all these popular and innovative methods, align their knowledge and understanding and map it on the design process.

How it works?

Visual tool based on the Double Diamond model is being developed. It explains key design terms and concepts and maps them on the design process. The tool works like a visual lexicon. In addition to that, we are developing a deck of cards - both digital and physical, that can be used by designers to plan and discuss their work according to their needs.

Why does it matter?

Whether it’s working together virtually or face to face, having a shared vocabulary allows for clear and concise communication between team members and clients, and also for efficient problem solving. Finally, having clear metrics in place makes it possible to measure the outcomes.

How can you use the tool?
Here are some tips:
  • E-learning and onboarding
  • Facilitating workshops
  • Meetings to align understanding within design teams
  • Overall mapping out the process

Create the right thing in the right way

The Double Diamond is a simple yet powerful way of mapping the design process. It has become a cornerstone of design language, used and referenced worldwide. The framework is aligned with the Design Thinking process. It consists of four phases organized in two diamond-shaped areas. The first one is dedicated to gathering information and user research. The second one is dedicated to processing information and the iterative design process.

The deck of cards

Use it on digital whiteboards or during face to face meetings

Each design concept or keyword mapped on the Double Diamond will have a corresponding digital or physical card. The purpose of these cards is to align and facilitate discussion around these concepts during virtual and face to face meetings. All the cards together create an asset library that can be imported to Miro, Mural or Figma and used for digital team cooperation. The deck of printed cards can be pre-ordered from the UX Campus.
